Housing market in the Netherlands


Housing shortage per province

North-Holland, Flevoland and Utrecht experiencing the highest shortage over 5%

{{js5|safe}} {{div5|safe}}


Despite an increased production of new homes, the housing shortage in the Netherlands increased from 3.8% in 2019 to 4.2% in 2020 driven by the strong growth of households amount. The shortage totaled 331 thousand houses in 2020. The situation is the most critical in provinces North-Holland, Flevoland and Utrecht where the shortage exceeded 5%. The housing shortage in the Netherlands is forecasted to go further up to 5.1% (419 thousand houses) in 2025.

Average house purchase prices per region

Haarlem, Gooi and Vechtstreek, and Amsterdam regions are the most expensive with prices exceeding €450k

{{js|safe}} {{div|safe}}

An average house purchase price in the Netherlands increased to € 311k in 2020. The most expensive regions are traditionally situated in Randstad and North- Brabant. The prices generally decrease as you get closer to the borders of the country. The cheapest regions are located in Groningen, Friesland, Drenthe, Limburg and Zeeland. The price difference between regions has grown wider over the years and amounts to € 320k in 2020. An average house purchase price in Haarlem agglomeration is almost 3 times higher than in Delfzij region.

An average house purchase price is calculated as an average of all transaction prices of existing houses sold in a period. The numbers are not adjusted for differences in houses characteristics. Differences between regions are a.o. caused by the type of houses that are sold. For example, an average sale price of a house in a region with relatively more villas will be higher than the one in a region with relatively more apartments.

Average monthly housing costs of tenants per region

Top-10 most expensive regions are situated in Randstad

{{js6|safe}} {{div6|safe}}

Housing costs of tenants include rent, energy, water and gas costs, municipal taxes, eventually reduced by a rent subsidy. Housing costs of tenants amounts to € 675 in 2018, an increase of 14% from 2012. Top-10 most expensive regions are all located in Randstad. The cheapest regions are mainly situated closer to the borders of the country, in provinces Groningen, Friesland, Drenthe, Limburg and Zeeland.
